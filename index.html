Helping research & biotech teams make decisions from omics data.

I support research groups and biotech teams with rigorous omics analysis and biological interpretation, turning complex datasets into clear conclusions that guide experimental next steps.

I work from study design and analysis planning through to decision focused interpretation, helping teams prioritise signals, avoid common pitfalls, and move projects forward with confidence. I also collaborate on bioAI projects, supporting the development and evaluation of AI-based methods for omics analysis and biological interpretation.

Who am I?

My name is Malte Borggrewe. I am a bioinformatics consultant and biomedical scientist with hands on experience in both wet lab research and computational analysis.

This dual background allows me to go beyond producing results. I focus on what is biologically meaningful, where uncertainty remains, and what the best next steps should be.

Projects & Publications

An overview of selected projects and publications.

Microglia development in human central nervous system

Understanding how human microglia develop and mature during fetal brain development.

  • Led bioinformatics analysis and biological interpretation across bulk and single cell datasets
  • Integrated transcriptomic and epigenomic data to identify developmental regulatory programs
  • Interpreted results in the context of human microglia development
  • Contributed to a peer reviewed publication

Published in Science

High-dimensional cytometry analysis toolkit

An R toolkit for the analysis of high-dimensional flow & mass cytometry data.

  • Designed and developed an R based toolkit for high dimensional flow and mass cytometry analysis
  • Implemented advanced data cleaning, preprocessing, and downstream analysis workflows
  • Integrated cytometry data with single cell RNA sequencing frameworks
  • Released the toolkit as open source software

Toolkit on Github

Glia function in neuroinflammation

Characterising astrocyte and microglia phenotypes during inflammatory processes in the central nervous system.

  • Led bulk RNA seq analysis to identify inflammation associated glial states
  • Quantified microglia phenotypes using morphology based analyses
  • Developed automated image analysis workflows for immunohistochemistry data

Publication in Glia and Acta Neuropathologica Communications
